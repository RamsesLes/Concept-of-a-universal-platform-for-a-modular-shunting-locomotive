

## Concept of a universal platform for a modular shunting locomotive
Upgrading the shunting locomotive fleet requires the use of cost-effective solutions both in locomotive design and operation. Locomotive customers must be provided with the best possible locomotive engineering solutions, while also considering their long-term financial capabilities. This will enable mutually beneficial cooperation with them.

## Shunting locomotives in the world
Shunting locomotives in the world are typically equipped with power plants and energy storage units, operating either separately or in combination. Some models can be connected to the overhead power system, which enables locomotive operation and recharging of the energy storage units. However, most locomotives operate with a diesel generator power plant and energy storage units operating in combination. While this design is no more efficient than a diesel engine, the accumulated design and operational experience in this area allows this design to be used without significant risks (both technical and economic).

Installing a pantograph requires safety precautions, as high voltage poses significant risks to maintenance personnel.

## Concept shunting locomotives
The proposed shunting locomotive concept will be built in GC gabarit, as this gauge is the most preferred for industrial applications and allows for optimal equipment configuration.

The power plant consists of a diesel generator and energy storage units. Optionally, the diesel generator can be replaced with a gas piston or hydrogen engine. To ensure cost-effective locomotive operation, several locomotive modifications are proposed, allowing for a more flexible approach to each customer's needs.

The axle arrangement is 1o-1o. A change to 2o-2o is possible upon refinement of the locomotive's specifications.

Power is 400 kW from a single diesel generator and 350 kW from energy storage units. The approximate values ​​shown here are for a locomotive configured with a diesel generator and energy storage units (hybrid configuration).

Traction force – approximately 128 kN

Axle load – approximately 22.5 t

Minimum curve radius – 60 m. With a 2o-2o design, the turning angles must be calculated, so the curve radius will be adjusted.

Equipment reserves – 2000 kg of diesel fuel, approximately 1000 kg of liquefied natural gas, 500 kg of sand.

Energy storage battery charging type: external charging – connecting the locomotive to a power outlet at the depot, or hot-swapping batteries. This means that charged batteries can be stored at the depot, ready for replacement.

Pantograph – connection to the overhead contact system, power supply for asynchronous traction motors, and charging of energy storage devices.

Diesel generator – power supply for asynchronous motors and charging of energy storage devices.

Recuperation – charging of batteries during braking.

Gas piston unit – power supply for asynchronous motors and charging of energy storage devices.

Hydrogen unit – charging of energy storage devices.

## Basic configuration of a shunting locomotive

The shunting locomotive will feature a bonnet-type body and a mainframe. A single frame platform will allow for several modifications, allowing for flexible selection of the shunting locomotive configuration to suit the customer's needs.

The basic version of the locomotive will include a diesel generator power plant, a pantograph, and asynchronous traction motors.

A fuel tank is mounted under the mainframe.

The frame will house modules—the powertrain, cooling system, equipment, and cab.

## Unified locomotive platform. Equipment

Implementation of a single platform with various locomotive options:

Frame, automatic coupler, track clearer, sand system - this will all be an unchangeable (or with minor changes) part of the locomotive

The fuel tank is located under the frame when installing a diesel generator power plant.

In the option where the locomotive uses only the energy of energy storage devices, this place can be taken by additional batteries (or a cooling system for traction electric motors).

When configured with a gas piston power plant, a container with a gas storage and regasification system will be installed under the frame.

The power module is located on the frame. Depending on the configuration, it will be equipped with a diesel generator or gas piston power plant.

Cooling module – installed together with diesel generator or gas piston power plants.

The hydrogen power unit module and the hydrogen storage system are installed together on the locomotive.

Equipment module – includes a compressor, a cooling system for traction asynchronous motors, energy storage devices (battery)

Current collector – additionally, high-voltage devices are placed in the equipment module to ensure the operation of the locomotive from the contact network.

## Unified locomotive platform. Locomotive control

Locomotive control can be implemented in three different ways, depending on customer requirements:

1. Control Cab. A cabin with control panels, a driver's seat, and side and windshield windows for visibility from the cabin.

2. Control console and wrist monitor. This control method is possible for smaller companies. Cost savings are achieved by eliminating the control cabin.

3. "Automotive Driver" System. This system allows one or more locomotives to be controlled from a single control station. This reduces crew changeover time and reduces the number of drivers (since a single depot driver can control the entire fleet).

## Scalability

To ensure traction performance, the locomotive can operate alone or in tandem with several other locomotives. Locomotives can be coupled and operated using a multiple-unit system. This allows for the necessary traction force to be generated for shunting operations.

The train's dispatcher will connect the pneumatic lines and electrical harnesses. Automation is also possible, with a manipulator mounted on the locomotive's frame connecting the locomotive systems to one another.
